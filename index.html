The one thing

how to not get scammed

in korea, literally any doctor can open a "skin clinic." only about 2% are actually board-certified dermatologists.

the rest are GPs, family-medicine docs, even OB-GYNs running laser machines. not always bad—plenty are fine for a basic aqua peel. but for anything that needs real judgment (scars, pigment, lasers on ur face) u want the real deal.

the magic words are 피부과 전문의—"board-certified dermatologist." it's on their korean site and the doctor's wall. if a clinic dodges the question when u ask, that IS ur answer.

doctor-led boutique

  • actual 1:1 time with the doctor
  • might talk u out of something
  • private rooms, plan made for u
  • worth it for scars, pigment, anything real
  • costs more but u get judgment

"factory" clinic

  • a "counselor" sells u before any doctor
  • shared rooms, self-cleansing, conveyor belt
  • u pick off a menu, no real diagnosis
  • watch for non-refundable deposits + upsell
  • fine if u know exactly what u want
pricing tip: screenshot prices off the UNNI app before u go—tourist quotes can be 2–3× what locals pay. a legit clinic puts the dose (cc/mL) in writing. a weirdly low price usually just means they're giving u less.

Seoul 101

first time in korea — the stuff that actually helps

not "go see a palace!" tips. the things that save u time + confusion in the first 48 hrs.

🗺️

download Naver Maps

not google maps, it lags hard on korean addresses. Naver Maps gets u to the exact building + floor. grab it before u land.

🚇

grab a T-money card at the airport

any CU or 7-Eleven in arrivals. works on every subway + bus. COEX = Samseong Station, Line 2 (green), Exit 5–6.

🚖

Kakao T for taxis

it's basically uber. drivers mostly don't speak english so just show them the destination in korean (copy it off the clinic's site).

🌡️

july is brutal

31°C, 85% humidity. get a handheld fan from Daiso day one (₩2–5k). then COEX AC is arctic inside, so bring a layer. ur welcome.

💳

cards mostly work

apple pay almost everywhere now. carry ₩50–100k cash for small spots. and no tipping anywhere—it's just not a thing here, don't make it weird.

🚪

exit numbers actually matter

one station can have 15+ exits spread over blocks. wrong exit = a 15-min walk in the heat. check the exit on Naver before u come up.

✈️

before u leave: K-ETA

US passport holders need a K-ETA (travel auth)—$10, online at k-eta.go.kr, takes up to 72h, good for 2 yrs. not a visa but u can't board without it. do it a week out.

handy at the clinic: screenshot 저는 민감성 피부예요 ("i have sensitive skin") and 처음이에요 ("this is my first time"). they'll appreciate the effort and it sets expectations.
