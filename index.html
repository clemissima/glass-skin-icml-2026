ICML 2026 · Seoul · Jul 6–11

Glass Skin
Between Sessions

You're going to Seoul to present your work. You're also going to be in Gangnam for six days—which happens to have the highest concentration of dermatology clinics on earth. This guide covers the overlap.

by @clemissima

Seoul in July is 31°C and 85% humidity. COEX air conditioning is industrial and aggressive. Your skin will be sweating on the walk in and desiccating on the conference floor—both things are true simultaneously.

You'll also be photographed at your poster with 8 minutes notice. The goal of this guide is to sequence treatments so you peak Thursday main conference—look fine by Wednesday, don't arrive Monday with a red face from something you did too aggressively on Sunday.

Sequencing

How to Play It

Seoul clinics run on same-day or next-day booking—you can realistically show up within 24 hours at most mid-tier clinics in Apgujeong or around COEX. That's the good news. The sequencing matters more than the specific clinic.

Arrival

Hold. Let your skin normalize.

You land Sunday morning. Drink water, don't touch your face, don't book anything for Sunday. Jetlag makes skin reactive—injectables and lasers on day one are a bad idea. Cleanser, sheet mask, SPF. That's it. Give it 24 hours.

Mon – Tue

Hydration foundation

First two days are for low-drama treatments: aqua peeling, hydra facial, Rejuran S booster (the lighter-touch version). These leave you glowy, not red. Book a consultation Monday afternoon after tutorials if you're feeling human. Walk-ins are generally accepted at most clinics by 4pm.

Wednesday

Light energy or nothing

If you want LED phototherapy or a low-fluence HIFU facial (the kind with zero downtime), Wednesday is your window. Avoid anything that leaves you pink for more than a few hours—you have two full conference days ahead. Also a fine day to skip entirely and be at the booth.

Fri – Sat

Push harder if you want

Main conference ends Thursday. If you want anything more aggressive—Profhilo, Plinest, proper Ulthera—Friday is your day. You have the weekend to recover before your flight Sunday. Don't plan anything aggressive Thursday night; workshops are lower intensity but you're still on.

Important

  • Avoid anything with 3+ days downtime before you present. No fractional lasers, no deep peels, nothing that leaves visible redness the next morning
  • Most clinics will ask if you're flying soon—answer honestly. Altitude + fresh injectables doesn't mix. Plan your last injectable day at least 72h before your return flight
  • Don't book an aggressive laser the night before your poster session. "The consultant said minimal downtime" does not account for conference-center lighting and a photographer. Save it for Friday
Clinics

Where to Go

Specifically: clinics near COEX that do non-invasive skin treatments, with English-capable staff or LINE consultations, at prices that won't require a separate budget line.

1

Lamiche Dermatology

Apgujeong · 18 min walk No downtime Book ahead

The answer when researchers ask where to start. Specializes in Rejuran (PDRN skin boosters) and aqua peeling stacks—you can walk in looking tired and leave looking like you slept. Their consultants are enthusiastic about stacking; you don't have to do everything in one session. If they say there's no availability to split, push—usually there is. English consultation available, LINE booking works fine.

2

PPEUM Aesthetic Clinic

Samsung-dong · 5 min walk No downtime

Closest to the venue—five minutes from the COEX main entrance. Useful if you want to sneak out between sessions for a 45-minute treatment. Their focus is skin texture: pores, brightness, tone. Results are subtle and immediate, which is exactly what you want mid-conference. Doesn't do dramatic volume work—not what you're here for anyway.

3

Arumdaun Nara Skin Clinic

Apgujeong · high-volume Korean-local

A lot of foreigners overlook this one because it doesn't market in English—the trade is a translation sheet and an assembly-line pace, but the technicians are excellent and prices are genuinely lower. Good for Glutathione IV drips, basic skin boosters, vitamin drips. Book through Naver or walk in at opening.

4

JW Dermatology

Gangnam-gu English staff Book ahead

If you want someone who will explain what they're doing in full sentences, JW has English-speaking consultants used to first-time patients. More expensive than the local clinics—you're paying for the hand-holding. Particularly good for people who haven't done Korean skin treatments before and want to ask questions before someone injects things into their face. Consultation is free if you book a treatment.

Logistics

Pack vs. Buy in Seoul

General rule: sunscreen, actives, and sheet masks are dramatically cheaper in Seoul. Don't fill your carry-on. Do pack: whatever prescription skincare you use, lip balm, any fragrance-free formulas you rely on for sensitivity.

Skip or ship home: Heavy oils, thick glass jars, vitamin C serums (oxidize in transit), most "viral K-beauty" products that are usually overhyped once you're actually there.

Shopping

Olive Young

It's a pharmacy-meets-beauty-store chain and it is everywhere. There's one 400m from COEX, another inside COEX itself. Think CVS if CVS were good. The selection is enormous and prices are real, not tourist markup. Best move is to walk the aisles Tuesday or Wednesday once you've settled in. The SPF wall alone is worth it.

Actual useful finds: Beauty of Joseon Relief Sun (rice + probiotics, no white cast), Skin1004 Madagascar Centella Asiatica toner, COSRX Snail 96 Mucin Power Essence, Dr. Jart+ Cicapair Tiger Grass Serum, Round Lab 1025 Dokdo Toner. All available online post-trip but 30–50% cheaper in store.

Staff won't approach you unless you ask. If you want a rec, show them your face on your phone with a translated note ("my skin is dry / oily / sensitive"). It works, genuinely.

Week-Of

Conference Week Basics

The goal is not to overdo it. Korean routines can be 10 steps—yours this week is 4: cleanser, toner or essence, SPF in the morning, sheet mask at night. Your skin needs to be stable while you're treating and presenting, not in an adjustment cycle.

COEX air conditioning is aggressive. Carry a face mist and actually use it—Klairs Fundamental Ampule Mist or any hydrating mist from Olive Young. The sheet mask at night genuinely matters; don't skip it on the days you present.

If you want minimal coverage for poster nights or dinner: a tinted SPF from Korea is the move. Light, non-greasy, photographs well under conference lighting. Nothing reads worse than full-coverage Western foundation over conference-dry skin.
